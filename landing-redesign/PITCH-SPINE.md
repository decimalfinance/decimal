# Decimal — pitch spine

The shared core for the YC app, the SPC app, and the landing hero. Written plain
(no marketing-speak, no em-dashes) per house voice. Claims are gated: the "what's
real today" ledger below keeps the landing page honest while the YC pitch can sell
the full vision.

---

## One-liner (three lengths)

**10-word "what we make" (YC's first question, plain):**
> AI accounts payable for US companies that pay teams abroad.

**Standard one-liner (company / investor):**
> Decimal is AI-powered accounts payable for US companies with overseas teams. It
> reads, codes, and approves every bill, then settles cross-border in stablecoins,
> so vendors get paid in minutes instead of days, without the 3 to 5 percent a bank
> buries in the exchange rate.

**Positioning line (the memorable version):**
> Your overseas team, paid like they're across the street.

## Who it's for (ICP)

> US digital, marketing, and dev agencies with a retained offshore team in the
> Philippines, India, or Eastern Europe. They pay 5 to 20 contractors every month,
> lose 3 to 5 percent plus several days to Wise, Payoneer, or PayPal, and match every
> foreign payout into QuickBooks by hand.

## The problem (2 sentences)

> A US company with an offshore team pays it through tools built for the last era of
> money: bank wires that take days and cost up to 15 percent all-in, or fintech apps
> that still clear through correspondent banks and mark up the exchange rate. The bill
> itself still gets typed, coded, chased for approval, and reconciled by a person,
> because none of those tools is actually accounts-payable software.

## Why now (the dated shift)

> Three things turned in 2025 and 2026. Stablecoins became a real settlement rail:
> Visa is settling billions in USDC, Stripe's Bridge got a bank charter, and B2B
> stablecoin payments hit 226 billion dollars in 2025, up more than seven times year
> over year. The GENIUS Act (July 2025) gave payment stablecoins federal legal
> standing, reserve rules, and redemption rights. And the people getting paid already
> want it: Deel, Upwork, and Fiverr all added USDC payouts because contractors in
> Argentina, Nigeria, and the Philippines prefer dollars that arrive in minutes.
> (YC-specific beat: YC now funds its own companies in USDC on Solana and is telling
> founders to build cross-border stablecoin payments. We are building exactly that.)

## Why us / the wedge (the market gap)

> The market is split and nobody owns the middle. Ramp, BILL, and Brex are excellent
> US-domestic AP with no real cross-border rail. Deel and Remote solve employment
> compliance but bundle per-seat pricing you don't need just to pay a contractor.
> Wise and Payoneer move money but aren't AP software, so the bill still gets coded,
> approved, and reconciled by hand. Decimal is the one product that does all three:
> the AP workflow, the cheap instant cross-border payment, and books that reconcile
> themselves. The stablecoin rail makes the payment 10x cheaper and faster; the AI
> handles the judgment work that rigid software can't; and because the money stays in
> an account only the customer controls, the automation is safe to trust.

## What's real today vs. what's next (the honesty ledger)

Keep every landing-page claim on the "today" side of this line.

- **Real today (devnet):** AI invoice capture and extraction; AI GL coding that learns
  each vendor's account; code-enforced approvals, spending limits, duplicate/payable
  gates; self-custodial treasury; on-chain USDC treasury payments and an agent that
  proposes and executes them under signed limits; two-way QuickBooks sync.
- **Not built yet (the hard part):** production payout rails and local-currency
  off-ramp to a contractor's bank. So "paid in their own currency to their bank in
  minutes" is the VISION, safe to pitch to YC as what we're building, but NOT a claim
  the landing page can state as shipped. On the landing page, sell the AP + coding +
  self-custody that IS real, and frame cross-border payout as the direction.

## Founder hook (for YC / SPC)

> A 22-year-old technical founder who, alone, shipped a working AI cross-border AP
> product settling in USDC on Solana, and earned a Solana Foundation grant before
> writing a pitch deck. Solo by choice: the bar for a cofounder is high, and nothing
> so far has needed to wait for one. Building the exact category YC is asking founders
> to build, on the exact rail YC now uses to pay its own companies.

## The one open decision for the landing hero

The ICP's whole pain is the payment, but the payout rail isn't shipped. So the landing
hero has two honest options:
- **(A) Sell the vision, mark it coming.** Hero promises the cross-border payment; a
  small honest signal ("payouts rolling out / join the waitlist") keeps it truthful.
  Strongest pull, needs the "coming" tell to stay honest.
- **(B) Sell what's shipped.** Hero leads on the AI AP + coding + self-custody that's
  real today; cross-border is framed as where it's going. Fully honest now, less punchy.
Recommendation: (A) for a pre-launch landing whose job is waitlist + YC credibility,
as long as the "coming" signal is genuinely visible. Decide before the hero copy.
