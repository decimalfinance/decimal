# Positioning — corrected source of truth for the landing redesign

Date: 2026-07-15. **This supersedes the framing in `PITCH-SPINE.md` and `GTM-SYNTHESIS.md`**
for all landing copy. Those two docs are contaminated with two rejected ideas:
(1) a contractor/"pay teams abroad" ICP, and (2) an "A/B overlap sweet spot" that narrows
the two customers into their intersection. Both are wrong. Mine those docs only for the
still-valid material flagged below; take positioning from here.

---

## The product in one sentence

**Decimal is AI-powered accounts payable software for businesses that pay vendors — locally and globally.**

## Non-negotiable framing rules

1. **Vendors, never contractors / employees / "teams."** AP = paying vendor invoices for goods
   and services. It is not payroll and not 1099 contractor pay. Any line about paying an
   "overseas team" or "contractors" is off-product and must not appear.
2. **One unified product.** Paying globally is a **feature / part** of the same product, not a
   separate solution or a separate audience. **Never split the page into two customer tracks.**
3. **The page sells the dream AND the work.** It presents the full product vision (including
   cross-border vendor payment) as what Decimal is/does — whether a piece is shipped yet does
   not gate the aspirational framing. The one guardrail: **do not fabricate live proof** — no
   invented metrics, customer counts, logos, or testimonials; no demo that pretends an unbuilt
   step already executed live. Product vision = fine. Fake present-tense evidence = not.
4. **No crypto jargon to the buyer.** Sell outcomes (fast, cheap, transparent, safe), not
   "stablecoins / multisig / web3." The real differentiator is control: **your money stays in an
   account only you control, and the rules can't be overridden — not by us, not by an insider.**
   Phrase it plainly; "self-custodial / on-chain" beats the weak "enforced by code."
5. **Plain voice, no em-dashes, Mercury-clean.** Human, not AI-marketing. Text fills its column.

## Who it's for — INTERNAL targeting lens only (never shown as a split)

Two customer shapes, **same product**:
- **Shape 1 — AP-heavy, complex orgs.** Drowning in vendor invoices and approval cycles;
  complicated org/approval structures. Construction, healthcare, inventory/warehouse,
  distribution, manufacturing, real estate. Value = AI removes the manual AP hours and tames
  approval chaos.
- **Shape 2 — global vendor payers.** Pay vendors across borders; the rail replaces slow,
  expensive international bank transfers. Value = cheaper, faster, transparent cross-border
  vendor payment.

Most real businesses are some of both. The page speaks to the **shared pain** (vendor AP is
manual, slow, error-prone; approvals are chaos) and presents global vendor payment as a natural
capability of the one product — not a second pitch.

## The problem (unified, vendor-framed)

Businesses that pay vendors still type invoices, code them to the books by hand, chase approvals
across email and Slack, and reconcile by hand. When those vendors are overseas, the payment
itself is slow and expensive: bank wires that take days at up to ~15% all-in, or fintech apps
that still clear through correspondent banks and mark up the exchange rate. No single tool does
the AP work well AND pays the vendor well.

## Why now (valid; keep — drop the contractor beat)

The settlement rail turned real in 2025–2026: Visa settling billions in USDC, Stripe's Bridge
got a bank charter, B2B stablecoin payments hit ~$226B in 2025 (+733% YoY). The GENIUS Act
(July 2025) gave payment stablecoins federal legal standing. (Accelerator context, not landing
copy: YC is actively pushing founders toward cross-border stablecoin payments.) **Cite only
these; avoid the inflated "$33T > Visa+MC" figure.**

## Why us / the gap (valid, from GTM-SYNTHESIS)

The market splits and leaves one lane open: enterprise suites (Coupa/Ariba — slow, rule-based,
AI is mostly OCR); mid-market ERP-native tools (Ramp/BILL/Stampli — fast but shallow, weak or no
global payout); Tipalti (real global reach but built outside the ERP → reconciliation lag +
opaque, multi-day, correspondent-bank FX); new AI-native entrants (all on traditional bank
rails). **Decimal's lane:** AI-native (an agent that *resolves* exceptions, not just routes them)
+ ERP-native + fast, transparent cross-border settlement + self-custodial control.

## The differentiator, in buyer language

- **AI that does the judgment work** — reads every invoice, codes it to your books, learns each
  vendor. Not just OCR.
- **An approval flow you design** — conditions and who-to-chase, plus policy toggles you control.
  (This is the strongest *shipped, demoable* feature — lean on it hard.)
- **Global vendor payments that clear in minutes** at a fraction of bank cost, with FX you can see.
- **Your money, your control** — funds sit in an account only you control; the rules can't be
  flipped by us or an insider.

## Shipped vs. vision (internal — the page sells both, honestly)

- **Shipped / demoable now:** invoice capture + AI extraction; AI GL coding (learns each vendor);
  the build-your-own approval engine + Policies page; QuickBooks two-way sync.
- **Open / greenfield:** the actual payment execution, the cross-border payout rail, and any
  settlement record/proof. Treasury (Squads) is untouched for ~a month and unconfirmed. The page
  may present cross-border payment as the product's promise — just no fabricated live proof.
  (See memory `project_payments_open`.)
