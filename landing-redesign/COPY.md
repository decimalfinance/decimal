## Notes for the copy's own sake

- Total hero text is ~20 words + one line. Density lives below the fold, in short leads +
  product visuals — inverted from v1 (rule 7).
- Iconic lines doing the work: "Self-driving accounts payable." / "It reads. It codes. It
  routes. You approve." / "Describe your approvals. Watch them take shape." / "Every vendor
  paid like they're next door." / "The AI works for you. The money answers to you." Each is a
  claim or a feeling, not a description.
- The complex-approval-workflow builder is a real, SHIPPED edge (Zaid) — most AP/finance
  software still can't give a good experience for building complex workflows. Section 3 owns
  it. The human AUTHORS the flow by describing it in plain words; the AI lays it out on the
  canvas (natural-language-to-canvas is roadmap, sold as built). Do NOT say "built by AI".
- Voice rules reinforced 2026-07-16 (Zaid): no negation, don't name the thing we're better
  than (e.g. "templates"), don't over-explain mechanics. Assert positively and flex the AI.
- What we deliberately do NOT have anymore: a problem section, a credibility/trust-chip
  strip, and a feature block per pipeline stage (the Brex catalog anti-pattern).
