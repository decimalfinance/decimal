# GTM synthesis (corrected) — AP-heavy customer map

Date: 2026-07-14. Supersedes the earlier version that was built on a wrong
(contractor-payment) ICP. Draws on `RESEARCH-customer-and-gtm.md` (§1 market, §5
accelerators still valid) plus three corrected threads: AP-heavy verticals, the
cross-border AP subset, and the competitive/GTM landscape.

Unifying thesis (Zaid): **Decimal is for AP-heavy businesses — many vendors, high
invoice volume — drowning in manual or legacy AP. If we save them serious time and
money, they're our customer.** Two overlapping profiles, both real; we don't narrow
away either, but they point at different verticals and different first-move realism.

---

## The two profiles map to different verticals

**Profile B — AP-heavy, legacy, domestic-ish (AI + upgrade is the pitch).**
Ranked AP-heavy verticals: (1) Construction (400–600 invoices/mo, job-cost coding +
retainage + sub-compliance, stuck on Sage 300 CRE / Foundation / Viewpoint; 66% still
manual), (2) Healthcare mid-size (tens of thousands of invoices/mo, GPO tiered pricing
breaks 3-way match, staffing shortage; 70% touchless cited), (3) Manufacturing
mid-market (68% still key by hand, 3.8x productivity ROI), (4) Real estate / property
mgmt (multi-entity tagging is the crisp pain), (5) Distribution, (6) Hospitality.
These mostly pay DOMESTIC vendors, so the stablecoin rail barely applies — the pitch
is pure AI + legacy-upgrade + time saved.

**Profile A — AP-heavy AND cross-border (stablecoin is the wedge).**
Ranked: (1) mid-market apparel/footwear/furniture importers sourcing Vietnam/China/
Indonesia (30/70 deposit-balance on repeat POs, $10k–300k, ~100% overseas vendors),
(2) electronics/components importers, (3) furniture, (4) nearshoring→Mexico. AVOID
LC-heavy segments (commodity traders, big construction-materials importers): letters
of credit serve a counterparty-GUARANTEE function, not settlement speed, so a
stablecoin rail competes on the wrong axis there.

**The overlap = the sweet spot:** import-heavy mid-market manufacturers and
importer/distributors. Both true at once — heavy vendor AP AND a global vendor base.

## The differentiated position (the gap nobody fills)

Incumbents split three ways and leave one lane open:
- Enterprise suites (SAP Ariba, Coupa, Basware): workflow/rule-based, 9–18 month
  implementations, AI is mostly OCR, can't autonomously resolve exceptions.
- Mid-market ERP-native tools (AvidXchange, BILL, Ramp, Stampli): fast + ERP-synced
  but SHALLOW or no global payout.
- The one global-payout leader (Tipalti): real 200-country reach, but built OUTSIDE
  the ERP → reconciliation lag + opaque, multi-day, correspondent-bank FX (its own
  reviews complain about exactly this).
- New AI-native entrants (Mod AI, Finto, ProcIndex — YC-backed): attacking AI-native
  AP, but all on traditional bank rails. None built on stablecoin/self-custody. **That
  is Decimal's open lane.**

**Decimal's position:** the one AP product that is AI-native (an agent that *resolves*
exceptions, not just routes them) + ERP-native (no reconciliation lag) + settles
cross-border fast with transparent FX (stablecoin rail, sold invisibly) + self-custodial.
Most valuable exactly where Tipalti is weakest: heavy cross-border vendor payments.

## Sell the rail INVISIBLY (hard buyer-resistance data)

77% of CFOs cite compliance uncertainty as the top barrier to crypto; only 13% use
stablecoins; 43% fear "volatility" even on dollar-pegged coins. So NEVER pitch "adopt
stablecoins." Pitch the outcome: "your AP tool pays your Shenzhen supplier and it
clears in 2 minutes at 0.3%, not 3 days at 3%." Matches Decimal's no-jargon rule.
Defensible stats to cite: Artemis — 62% of stablecoin users cite paying suppliers;
$226B B2B stablecoin volume 2025 (+733% YoY). AVOID vendor-blog percentages and the
inflated "$33T > Visa+MC" line.

## GTM reality — mid-market beachhead, NOT enterprise

Enterprise AP is a 6–18 month, 4–8-stakeholder sale with mandatory security review,
SOC 2, ERP-partner certifications, and reference customers — NOT survivable for a solo
pre-launch founder. The realistic beachhead is **mid-market**: companies (a) on a
modern-enough ERP (NetSuite / QuickBooks / Sage Intacct, not raw SAP) so integration
is tractable, (b) with real cross-border vendor volume so the FX savings are provable
in dollars, (c) currently on BILL/Ramp/manual/Tipalti and visibly outgrowing it, where
a Controller or founder can approve unilaterally (90–180 day cycle, compressible for a
design-partner deal). First design partners: leverage existing Superteam/Solana grant
+ ecosystem relationships and personalized outbound to Controllers/AP managers at
cross-border-exposed companies; paid design partnerships, not free pilots.

## Recommended shape

- **Beachhead ICP (first customers):** mid-market import-heavy businesses — importers,
  distributors, and import-sourcing brands/manufacturers (apparel/footwear/furniture/
  electronics/goods) on NetSuite/QBO/Sage Intacct, paying many overseas suppliers,
  outgrowing BILL/Ramp/manual. This is the A/B overlap: heavy AP + provable stablecoin
  wedge + reachable buyer.
- **Vision / TAM (the bigger story):** all AP-heavy businesses drowning in legacy AP
  (construction, healthcare, manufacturing, real estate). Domestic-heavy, enterprise-
  ish, second-wave — great for the "why this is huge" narrative, not the first sale.
- **Accelerators (unchanged, time-critical):** YC F26 deadline Jul 27; SPC Aug 2; a16z
  CSX (not Speedrun) opportunistic. Founder narrative per `RESEARCH-customer-and-gtm.md §5`.
