# Bridge

- Category: orchestration, wallets, issuance, cross-border payments, cards
- Stage: acquired by Stripe in 2025
- Main buyer: fintechs, global businesses, developers, treasury and payout teams

## What they build

- A stablecoin orchestration layer that abstracts over wallets, movement of funds, cards, issuance, and cross-border payment flows.
- The product positioning is explicitly about reducing blockchain complexity for businesses that want stablecoin rails without operating raw crypto infrastructure themselves.

## Core customer problem

- Businesses do not want to manage chain-level complexity, custody flows, or fragmented payment rails directly.
- They want one programmable layer for moving value between fiat, stablecoins, and destination networks.

## Why this matters

- This is one of the clearest proofs that the market wants managed stablecoin infrastructure rather than raw blockchain access.
- If Bridge is right, the best products are not “wallet plus explorer.” They are operating systems for money movement.

## Business shape

- Strong enterprise / platform wedge.
- Likely monetization through API usage, payment volume, wallet / card features, and higher-trust money movement flows.

## Research signals

- Important reference for any idea around:
  - routing
  - payouts
  - wallet infra
  - card backends
  - cross-border treasury
