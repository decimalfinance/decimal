# Project Scorecard

As of April 1, 2026, this scorecard evaluates the current project thesis:

`Stablecoin Ops Control Surface` for Solana, focused on request-to-settlement matching, reconciliation, exception handling, and treasury workflow for teams already moving USDC.

| Dimension | Score / 10 | Short Read |
|---|---:|---|
| Novelty | 7 | Not blank-space territory, but the exact `stablecoin ops + reconciliation + treasury control` intersection is less common than generic payment rails. |
| Market Timing | 9 | Strong timing. Stablecoin treasury and ops infrastructure is becoming more important now. |
| Competitive Gap | 7 | Payment rails are crowded, but the operational control and reconciliation layer above them is less saturated. |
| Mechanism Design | 8 | Strong if kept narrow: request intent, trusted destinations, observed movement matching, and exception queues. |
| Accelerator Overlap | 5 | Real overlap exists, mostly in payment rails, remittance, and consumer spend, not directly in ops assurance. |
| Hackathon Precedent | 8 | Strong precedent across Stablecoins and Infrastructure tracks, which helps legibility and credibility. |
| Archive Backing | 9 | Very strong support from treasury, indexing, reconciliation, auditability, and stablecoin market-structure sources. |
| Builder Density at Intersection | 6 | Nearby areas are dense, but the exact intersection is only medium-density rather than fully crowded. |

| Overall | Score |
|---|---:|
| Weighted read | 7.4 / 10 |

## Interpretation

- This is a good project.
- It is not a blank-space idea.
- It is strongest when positioned as `ops assurance + reconciliation infrastructure`.
- It is weaker when positioned as `another stablecoin payments platform`.
